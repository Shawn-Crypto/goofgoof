Preamble and Recitals

WHEREAS the Company is engaged in the business of providing specialized digital educational services through proprietary technological platforms and methodologies;

AND WHEREAS the Company has developed, created, and owns intellectual property rights in and to certain educational curriculum, methodologies, assessments, and related materials collectively referred to as the "Beyond the Deck" educational program;

AND WHEREAS the Company desires to establish comprehensive terms and conditions governing the circumstances under which pecuniary consideration paid by subscribers may be subject to refund, restitution, or reversal;

NOW THEREFORE the Company hereby establishes this Policy for Refund, Restitution and Reversal of Pecuniary Consideration ("Policy") which shall govern all subscription agreements and related transactions.

Article I: Definitions, Interpretations and Construction

1.1 Definitional Framework

For the purposes of this Policy, and unless the context otherwise requires or admits of a different construction, the following expressions shall bear the meanings hereinafter respectively assigned to them:

(a) "Subscriber" shall mean and include any natural person, Hindu Undivided Family, company, partnership firm, limited liability partnership, society, trust, or any other legal entity, whether incorporated or unincorporated, resident or non-resident, who has entered into a Subscription Agreement with the Company, and shall include such person's successors-in-interest, assigns, legal representatives, heirs, executors, administrators, and permitted transferees;

(b) "Subscription Agreement" shall mean and include the legally binding contractual arrangement executed between the Subscriber and the Company, whether electronically or otherwise, for the provision of Digital Educational Services, and shall include all amendments, modifications, addenda, schedules, annexures, and supplementary agreements thereto, and shall incorporate by reference this Policy, the Terms and Conditions of Service, Privacy Policy, Intellectual Property Policy, and all other policies of the Company as may be applicable from time to time;

(c) "Digital Educational Services" shall mean and include the entirety of educational content, curriculum, modules, lessons, assessments, examinations, interactive elements, multimedia presentations, downloadable resources, supplementary materials, community access, forum participation rights, certification programs, and all other services, facilities, and benefits provided by the Company through its digital platform, whether existing as of the date of subscription or subsequently added, modified, or enhanced;

(d) "Mandatory Qualifying Engagement Protocol" shall mean the compulsory preliminary evaluation period of seven (7) consecutive calendar days commencing from the date and time of initial platform access by the Subscriber, during which period the Subscriber is required to complete and fulfill all obligations set forth in sub-clause (e) hereof;

(e) "Foundation Module Completion Requirements" shall mean the successful completion, in their entirety and in sequential order, of the first three (3) educational modules designated as foundation modules, including but not limited to: (i) all video content consumption; (ii) all reading materials review; (iii) all interactive exercises participation; (iv) all assessments and evaluations attempted with minimum passing scores; (v) all discussion forum contributions as mandated; and (vi) all other activities, exercises, or requirements as may be prescribed within such foundation modules;

(f) "Minimum Engagement Threshold" shall mean the completion of not less than eight (8) aggregate hours of documented platform interaction and engagement, as measured and recorded by the Company's technological infrastructure, tracking systems, and analytics tools, which aggregate hours shall include only active engagement and shall exclude idle time, paused content, or inactive platform presence;

(g) "Disqualifying Events" shall mean any of the circumstances, actions, or omissions set forth in Article III hereof, the occurrence of any one or more of which shall render the Subscriber absolutely and irrevocably ineligible for any refund, restitution, or reversal of pecuniary consideration;

(h) "Pecuniary Consideration" shall mean all amounts paid or payable by the Subscriber to the Company in connection with the Subscription Agreement, including but not limited to subscription fees, taxes, processing charges, platform fees, and any other charges or levies;

(i) "Proprietary Materials" shall mean all content, materials, information, data, methodologies, processes, techniques, know-how, trade secrets, and intellectual property of whatsoever nature contained within or forming part of the Digital Educational Services;

(j) "Arbitral Proceedings" shall mean binding arbitration conducted in accordance with the provisions of the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 2015, and the rules and procedures set forth in Article VII hereof.

Article II: Eligibility Matrix for Refund Consideration

2.1 Fundamental Prerequisites for Refund Eligibility

No Subscriber shall be entitled to claim, demand, or receive any refund, restitution, or reversal of pecuniary consideration unless and until such Subscriber has satisfied each and every one of the following cumulative conditions precedent, the satisfaction of which shall be determined solely at the discretion of the Company:

(a) Temporal Compliance Requirement: The Subscriber shall have completed the entire duration of the Mandatory Qualifying Engagement Protocol without interruption, suspension, or premature termination, and shall submit any application for refund consideration only during the period commencing on the eighth (8th) day following the date of purchase and ending on the thirtieth (30th) day following such date of purchase, both days inclusive;

(b) Educational Engagement Fulfillment: The Subscriber shall have completed, to the entire satisfaction of the Company, all Foundation Module Completion Requirements as evidenced by the Company's tracking and monitoring systems;

(c) Quantitative Engagement Verification: The Subscriber shall have satisfied the Minimum Engagement Threshold as recorded and verified by the Company's technological infrastructure;

(d) Procedural Compliance Demonstration: The Subscriber shall have complied with all procedural requirements set forth in Article IV hereof;

(e) Absence of Disqualifying Events: No Disqualifying Events shall have occurred in relation to the Subscriber or the Subscriber's use of the Digital Educational Services.

2.2 Substantive Grounds for Refund Consideration

Even upon satisfaction of all conditions precedent set forth in sub-clause 2.1 hereof, refund consideration shall be available solely upon demonstration by the Subscriber of specific, material, and substantial deficiencies in the Digital Educational Services, which deficiencies must:

(a) Relate specifically to content covered within the Foundation Module Completion Requirements;

(b) Be capable of objective verification and substantiation;

(c) Not be attributable to the Subscriber's technical limitations, equipment deficiencies, internet connectivity issues, or personal preferences;

(d) Constitute material deviations from the Company's express representations regarding the Digital Educational Services.

2.3 Discretionary Nature of Refund Determinations

Notwithstanding satisfaction of all conditions precedent and substantive grounds set forth hereinabove, the grant or denial of any refund shall remain within the absolute and unfettered discretion of the Company, and no Subscriber shall have any legal, equitable, or contractual right to compel the Company to grant any refund.

Article III: Absolute Disqualification Criteria

3.1 Temporal Disqualification Events

The following temporal events shall constitute Disqualifying Events rendering any Subscriber absolutely and irrevocably ineligible for refund consideration:

(a) Submission of refund application prior to the expiration of the Mandatory Qualifying Engagement Protocol;

(b) Submission of refund application subsequent to the expiration of thirty (30) calendar days from the date of purchase;

(c) Failure to complete the Mandatory Qualifying Engagement Protocol within the prescribed timeframe;

(d) Any interruption, suspension, or premature termination of the Mandatory Qualifying Engagement Protocol by the Subscriber.

3.2 Engagement-Based Disqualification Events

The following engagement-related events shall constitute Disqualifying Events:

(a) Failure to complete any component of the Foundation Module Completion Requirements;

(b) Failure to satisfy the Minimum Engagement Threshold;

(c) Any attempt to circumvent, bypass, or manipulate the Company's tracking and monitoring systems;

(d) Completion of educational modules beyond the foundation modules;

(e) Generation, issuance, or receipt of any certificate, credential, or recognition from the Company.

3.3 Usage-Based Disqualification Events

The following usage-related events shall constitute Disqualifying Events:

(a) Downloading, saving, copying, or otherwise obtaining offline access to any Proprietary Materials;

(b) Sharing, distributing, or providing access to the Digital Educational Services to any third party;

(c) Attempting to reverse engineer, decompile, or extract any Proprietary Materials;

(d) Violation of any intellectual property rights of the Company;

(e) Breach of any confidentiality or non-disclosure obligations.

3.4 Procedural Disqualification Events

The following procedural events shall constitute Disqualifying Events:

(a) Failure to comply with any procedural requirement set forth in Article IV hereof;

(b) Provision of false, misleading, or incomplete information in any refund application;

(c) Failure to respond to Company inquiries within prescribed timeframes;

(d) Institution of any legal proceedings against the Company prior to completion of dispute resolution procedures set forth herein.

Article IV: Procedural Requirements for Refund Applications

4.1 Mandatory Documentation and Submission Requirements

Any Subscriber seeking refund consideration shall comply with the following mandatory procedural requirements:

(a) Written Application: Submit a comprehensive written application via electronic mail to the designated address help@lfgventures.in, which application shall contain: (i) complete identification details of the Subscriber; (ii) transaction reference numbers and payment details; (iii) detailed chronological account of the Subscriber's engagement with the Digital Educational Services; (iv) specific identification of alleged deficiencies with reference to particular components of the Foundation Module Completion Requirements; (v) documentary evidence supporting all allegations; and (vi) confirmation of compliance with all conditions precedent;

(b) Supporting Documentation: Provide copies of all relevant documentation including but not limited to: (i) payment receipts and transaction confirmations; (ii) screen captures evidencing completion of Foundation Module Completion Requirements; (iii) platform engagement logs and activity reports; (iv) technical specifications of devices used for platform access; (v) internet connectivity details and service provider information;

(c) Acknowledgment and Undertakings: Execute and submit written acknowledgments and undertakings confirming: (i) understanding of the confidential and proprietary nature of all accessed materials; (ii) commitment to maintain confidentiality of all Proprietary Materials in perpetuity; (iii) undertaking not to use, reproduce, or distribute any accessed content; (iv) agreement to immediate termination of platform access upon refund processing.

4.2 Processing Fee and Administrative Charges

(a) Every refund application shall be accompanied by payment of a non-refundable administrative processing fee of ₹199/- (Rupees One Hundred and Ninety-Nine Only);

(b) Such processing fee shall be paid through the Company's designated payment channels;

(c) Applications submitted without payment of the processing fee shall be deemed incomplete and shall not be processed.

4.3 Company's Assessment and Evaluation Process

(a) Upon receipt of a complete refund application, the Company shall have up to twenty-one (21) business days to conduct its assessment and evaluation;

(b) The Company reserves the right to engage independent technical experts, educational consultants, and legal advisors for the purpose of such assessment;

(c) The Company may require the Subscriber to provide additional information, clarifications, or documentation as deemed necessary;

(d) The Company's decision regarding any refund application shall be final and binding, subject only to the dispute resolution procedures set forth in Article VII hereof.

Article V: Refund Processing and Settlement Provisions

5.1 Approved Refund Processing

In the event that the Company, in its sole discretion, approves a refund application:

(a) The administrative processing fee of ₹199/- shall be deducted from the refund amount;

(b) Additional administrative charges equal to ten percent (10%) of the original subscription amount shall be deducted;

(c) All payment gateway charges, processing fees, and transaction costs originally borne by the Company shall be deducted;

(d) The net refund amount shall be processed within fifteen (15) to twenty-one (21) business days of approval;

(e) Refunds shall be credited exclusively to the original payment method used for the subscription;

(f) The Subscriber's access to the Digital Educational Services shall be immediately and permanently terminated.

5.2 Currency and Exchange Rate Provisions

(a) All refunds shall be processed in Indian Rupees regardless of the original payment currency;

(b) Any currency conversion shall be at the exchange rate prevailing on the date of refund processing;

(c) All currency conversion costs and exchange rate fluctuation risks shall be borne exclusively by the Subscriber.

5.3 Tax Implications and Compliance

(a) The Subscriber shall be solely responsible for all tax implications arising from refund processing;

(b) The Company shall deduct any applicable taxes as required under Indian tax laws;

(c) The Subscriber acknowledges that refund processing may have tax consequences and agrees to seek independent tax advice.

Article VI: Intellectual Property Protection and Enforcement

6.1 Proprietary Rights Assertion

The Subscriber acknowledges and agrees that:

(a) All Proprietary Materials constitute valuable trade secrets and confidential information of the Company;

(b) The Company retains all right, title, and interest in and to the Proprietary Materials;

(c) No license, right, or interest in the Proprietary Materials is granted to the Subscriber other than the limited right to access during the subscription period.

6.2 Post-Refund Obligations

Upon processing of any refund, the Subscriber shall:

(a) Immediately cease all use of any Proprietary Materials;

(b) Delete, destroy, or return all copies of Proprietary Materials in the Subscriber's possession;

(c) Maintain perpetual confidentiality regarding all accessed content;

(d) Not use any knowledge or information derived from the Proprietary Materials for any commercial or competitive purpose.

6.3 Enforcement and Remedies

(a) Any violation of intellectual property rights shall entitle the Company to immediate injunctive relief;

(b) The Subscriber shall be liable for liquidated damages of ₹50,000/- for each instance of unauthorized use or disclosure;

(c) The Company reserves the right to pursue all available legal remedies including criminal prosecution under applicable intellectual property laws.

Article VII: Dispute Resolution and Arbitration

7.1 Mandatory Alternative Dispute Resolution

(a) All disputes, differences, claims, or controversies arising out of or in connection with this Policy, the Subscription Agreement, or the Digital Educational Services shall be resolved exclusively through binding arbitration;

(b) No Subscriber shall institute any legal proceedings in any court of law prior to completion of the arbitration process set forth herein;

(c) The arbitration shall be conducted in accordance with the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 2015.

7.2 Constitution of Arbitral Tribunal

(a) Disputes involving claims up to ₹10,00,000/- (Rupees Ten Lakhs) shall be decided by a sole arbitrator;

(b) Disputes involving claims exceeding ₹10,00,000/- shall be decided by a tribunal of three arbitrators;

(c) The arbitrator(s) shall be appointed by mutual agreement, or failing such agreement, by the Mumbai Centre for International Arbitration (MCIA);

(d) All arbitrators must be qualified advocates with minimum fifteen (15) years of practice experience.

7.3 Seat, Venue, and Procedural Rules

(a) The seat and venue of arbitration shall be Mumbai, Maharashtra, India;

(b) The language of arbitration shall be English;

(c) The arbitration shall be conducted on a fast-track basis with awards to be rendered within six (6) months;

(d) The rules of evidence shall be those applicable in Indian courts;

(e) Limited discovery shall be permitted at the discretion of the arbitral tribunal.

7.4 Costs and Attorney Fees

(a) Each party shall initially bear its own costs and expenses;

(b) The arbitral tribunal may award costs and attorney fees to the prevailing party;

(c) Frivolous or vexatious claims may result in punitive cost awards.
