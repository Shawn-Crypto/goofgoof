Preamble and Regulatory Framework

WHEREAS LFG Ventures LLP ("Company," "we," "us," or "our") serves as a Data Fiduciary under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 ("DPDP Act") and is committed to maintaining the highest standards of data protection, privacy safeguards, and regulatory compliance;

AND WHEREAS the Company operates sophisticated digital educational platforms, technological infrastructure, and data processing systems that necessitate comprehensive privacy and data protection frameworks;

AND WHEREAS the Company is subject to multiple regulatory frameworks including the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, Information Technology Act, 2000, Consumer Protection Act, 2019, and various sectoral regulations governing data processing activities;

AND WHEREAS it is imperative to establish comprehensive policies governing the collection, processing, storage, transfer, and deletion of personal data in accordance with applicable legal requirements and international best practices;

NOW THEREFORE the Company hereby establishes this Privacy and Data Protection Policy ("Policy") to govern all aspects of personal data handling in connection with its business operations, educational services, and technological infrastructure.

Article I: Definitions and Regulatory Interpretation

1.1 Statutory and Regulatory Definitions

For the purposes of this Policy, and in accordance with applicable data protection legislation, the following expressions shall bear the meanings assigned to them under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, and related regulations:

(a) "Personal Data" shall mean any data about an individual who is identifiable by or in relation to such data, including but not limited to name, contact information, demographic details, behavioral data, device identifiers, biometric information, financial records, educational records, and any other information that directly or indirectly identifies a natural person;

(b) "Sensitive Personal Data" shall mean personal data comprising financial information, health records, official identifiers, sex life details, sexual orientation, biometric data, genetic data, transgender status, intersex status, caste or tribe information, religious or political beliefs, or any other categories designated as sensitive under applicable law;

(c) "Data Principal" shall mean the individual to whom personal data relates, including prospective users, registered users, subscribers, course participants, and any other natural persons whose data is processed by the Company;

(d) "Data Fiduciary" shall mean the Company in its capacity as the entity that determines the purpose and means of processing personal data;

(e) "Data Processor" shall mean any entity that processes personal data on behalf of the Data Fiduciary, including third-party service providers, technology vendors, and business associates;

(f) "Processing" shall mean any operation or set of operations performed on personal data, including collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure, dissemination, restriction, erasure, or destruction;

(g) "Consent" shall mean any freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous indication of the Data Principal's agreement to the processing of personal data relating to them, as evidenced through affirmative action or clear opt-in mechanisms;

(h) "Cross-Border Data Transfer" shall mean transfer of personal data from India to any country outside India or to any international organization, whether through direct transmission, cloud storage, or third-party service provider arrangements.

1.2 Technical and Operational Definitions

(a) "Digital Educational Platform" shall mean the Company's proprietary technological infrastructure, including websites, mobile applications, learning management systems, content delivery networks, user portals, assessment systems, and all related technological components;

(b) "Tracking Technologies" shall mean cookies, pixels, web beacons, software development kits, application programming interfaces, device fingerprinting technologies, and any other technological mechanisms used to collect user interaction data;

(c) "Third-Party Data Recipients" shall mean external entities that receive personal data from the Company, including technology service providers, payment processors, analytics platforms, marketing service providers, and business partners;

(d) "Data Retention Framework" shall mean the systematic approach to determining appropriate retention periods for different categories of personal data based on legal requirements, business necessity, and Data Principal preferences;

(e) "Security Incident" shall mean any unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of personal data, including data breaches, cyber attacks, system compromises, and accidental data exposures.

ARTICLE III: LEGAL BASIS AND CONSENT FRAMEWORK

3.1 Multifaceted Legal Basis for Processing

3.1.1 Consent-Based Processing

Where processing is based on Data Principal consent, such consent shall be:

(a) Specific and Granular: Obtained for clearly defined processing purposes with separate consent mechanisms for different processing activities;

(b) Informed and Transparent: Accompanied by comprehensive information about processing purposes, data recipients, retention periods, and Data Principal rights;

(c) Freely Given: Obtained without coercion, deception, or bundling with unrelated services or benefits;

(d) Unambiguous: Evidenced through clear affirmative action including electronic acceptance, opt-in mechanisms, or explicit written agreement;

(e) Withdrawable: Subject to easy withdrawal mechanisms that do not prejudice the Data Principal's access to services where legally permissible.

3.1.2 Alternative Legal Basis Categories

The Company may process personal data based on alternative legal grounds including:

(a) Contractual Necessity: Processing required for performance of contracts with Data Principals or implementation of pre-contractual measures;

(b) Legal Compliance: Processing mandated by applicable laws, regulations, court orders, or regulatory requirements;

(c) Legitimate Business Interests: Processing necessary for legitimate business purposes that do not override Data Principal rights and freedoms;

(d) Vital Interests Protection: Processing necessary to protect vital interests of Data Principals or other natural persons;

(e) Public Interest: Processing required for tasks carried out in the public interest or in exercise of official authority.

3.2 Consent Management and Withdrawal Framework

3.2.1 Consent Acquisition Mechanisms

(a) Layered Consent Structures: Multi-tiered consent mechanisms providing both summary and detailed information about processing activities;

(b) Dynamic Consent Updates: Mechanisms for obtaining additional consent when processing purposes expand or change;

(c) Granular Consent Options: Separate consent requests for marketing communications, analytics, third-party data sharing, and advanced tracking;

(d) Consent Records Maintenance: Comprehensive documentation of when, how, and for what purposes consent was obtained.

3.2.2 Consent Withdrawal Procedures

Data Principals may withdraw consent through the following mechanisms:

(a) Electronic Withdrawal: Online portals, email communications, or platform-based preference management systems;

(b) Written Communication: Formal written requests submitted to the Company's designated contact addresses;

(c) Telephonic Requests: Voice-based consent withdrawal through verified customer service interactions;

(d) Automatic Compliance: Implementation of withdrawal requests within reasonable timeframes not exceeding thirty (30) days unless technical limitations require extended timelines.

ARTICLE V: DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS AND EXERCISE PROCEDURES

5.1 Comprehensive Rights Framework Under DPDP Act

5.1.1 Fundamental Rights Categories

Data Principals possess the following rights regarding their personal data:

(a) Right to Information: Comprehensive disclosure regarding data processing purposes, categories of data, recipients, retention periods, and Data Principal rights;

(b) Right of Access: Detailed information about specific personal data being processed, processing purposes, and data sharing arrangements;

(c) Right to Correction: Rectification of inaccurate, incomplete, or outdated personal data within reasonable timeframes;

(d) Right to Erasure: Deletion of personal data where processing is no longer necessary, consent is withdrawn, or processing is unlawful;

(e) Right to Data Portability: Provision of personal data in structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format for transfer to alternative service providers;

(f) Right to Grievance Redressal: Access to effective complaint mechanisms and resolution procedures for data protection concerns.

5.1.2 Enhanced Rights for Specific Circumstances

(a) Right to Object: Opposition to processing based on legitimate interests, direct marketing, or automated decision-making;

(b) Right to Restriction: Limitation of processing during dispute resolution, accuracy verification, or pending erasure decisions;

(c) Right to Nominate: Appointment of representatives for rights exercise in case of incapacity or death;

(d) Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making: Protection against solely automated decision-making with significant effects including profiling and algorithmic processing.

5.2 Rights Exercise Procedures and Requirements

5.2.1 Formal Request Mechanisms

Data Principals may exercise rights through the following verified procedures:

(a) Written Communications: Detailed written requests submitted via email to help@lfgventures.in or postal communication to the registered office address;

(b) Identity Verification Requirements: Provision of government-issued identification, account verification information, and additional authentication measures as reasonably required;

(c) Specific Request Documentation: Clear identification of the specific rights being exercised, relevant personal data categories, and desired outcomes;

(d) Supporting Documentation: Any additional information or documentation reasonably required to process the request effectively.

5.2.2 Response Timelines and Procedures

(a) Initial Acknowledgment: Written confirmation of request receipt within seventy-two (72) hours of submission;

(b) Substantive Response: Comprehensive response addressing the rights request within thirty (30) days of verified receipt;

(c) Complex Request Extensions: Additional thirty (30) day extensions for complex requests involving multiple data categories or technical challenges, with detailed explanation provided to Data Principal;

(d) Status Updates: Regular communication regarding request processing status and any additional information requirements.

5.3 Limitations and Restrictions on Rights Exercise

5.3.1 Legal and Technical Limitations

Rights exercise may be limited or refused where:

(a) Legal Compliance Requirements: Processing is mandated by applicable laws, regulations, or judicial orders;

(b) Contractual Obligations: Data retention is necessary for contract performance or dispute resolution;

(c) Technical Impossibility: Requests are technically unfeasible due to system limitations or disproportionate effort requirements;

(d) Third-Party Rights: Rights exercise would adversely affect rights and freedoms of other individuals;

(e) Business Legitimate Interests: Processing is necessary for compelling legitimate business interests that override Data Principal rights.

5.3.2 Fee Structure for Excessive Requests

(a) Reasonable Request Processing: Initial and reasonable requests processed without charge to Data Principals;

(b) Excessive Request Fees: Administrative fees may be charged for manifestly unfounded, excessive, or repetitive requests, calculated based on administrative costs and processing complexity;

(c) Fee Notification: Advance notification of applicable fees with option for Data Principal to withdraw or modify request;

(d) Fee Waiver Discretion: Company discretion to waive fees for legitimate Data Principal needs or special circumstances.
